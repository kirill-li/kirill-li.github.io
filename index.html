My Story

My name is Kirill. I'm 27 years old, from Bishkek, Kyrgyzstan. In summer 2025, I was diagnosed with Primary Mediastinal B-Cell Lymphoma (PMBCL) — an aggressive blood cancer. I fought through 6 cycles of DA-EPOCH-R + nivolumab chemotherapy and achieved remission, with only residual tumor burning out.

But the remission was short-lived. A new tumor appeared in my brain — left temporal lobe. CNS recurrence. This wasn't part of my original diagnosis. It was a new battle.

Recurrence

Shanghai, China
Came to GoBroad Healthcare specifically to treat the CNS recurrence. Alone, no family, no insurance, in a foreign country.
Treatment in China
One round of teniposide chemotherapy at 10x the standard dose. Stem cells successfully collected.
Now — Phase 2
Autologous stem cell transplant (ASCT) + dual CD19/CD20 CAR-T cell therapy — my best chance at survival.

I've already spent over $300,000 — all of my personal savings and assets on treatment. I need $110,000 to $140,000 more to complete the next phase. The hospital requires a minimum deposit of ~$70,000 before I can proceed.

The upcoming treatment will be extremely intensive — I will be living with virtually no immune system for nearly a month. I will also need funds for post-treatment recovery, monitoring, and follow-up examinations.

Time is critical. There's a limited window before the cancer can progress. I'm fighting this alone — no family here, no safety net. Every day matters.
