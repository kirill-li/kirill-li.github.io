My Story

My name is Kirill. I'm 27 years old, from Bishkek, Kyrgyzstan. In 2024, I was diagnosed with Primary Mediastinal B-Cell Lymphoma (PMBCL) — an aggressive blood cancer. Then came the worst news: the cancer had spread to my brain. A tumor in my left temporal lobe.

Diagnosis
PMBCL with CNS recurrence — aggressive lymphoma with brain metastasis (left temporal lobe tumor)
Treatment Location
Shanghai, China — GoBroad Healthcare. Alone, no family, no insurance, in a foreign country.
Phase 1 Complete
6 cycles of DA-EPOCH-R + nivolumab chemotherapy. Mediastinal mass is shrinking. Stem cells successfully collected.
Now — Phase 2
Autologous stem cell transplant (ASCT) + dual CD19/CD20 CAR-T cell therapy — my best chance at survival.

I've already spent approximately $300,000 on treatment. I need $110,000 to $140,000 more to complete the next phase. The hospital requires a minimum deposit of ~$70,000 before I can proceed.

Time is critical. There's a limited window before the cancer can progress. I'm fighting this alone — no family here, no safety net. Every day matters.
